Development of smart specialization of the Donetsk region. in the direction of Mechanical Engineering and Information Technologies

On June 1, 2021, the Eastern Ukrainian Technocluster State Enterprise took part in the meeting of the working group on the development of smart specialization of the Donetsk region in the direction of "Mechanical engineering using modern information technologies (IT)" as part of the "Transformation of regions through joint smart solutions" project. The meeting was held in Kramatorsk and Mariupol, and online communication was established between the cities.

Together with the experts of the Ukrainian Institute of International Policy, the Joint Research Center, the EU Science Center and other students of the working group, they formed a SWOT matrix of the Donetsk region at the intersection of two industries: Mechanical Engineering and Information Technologies.

Further, the work is based on researching narrow niches and conducting cross-sectoral meetings. The development of smart specialization takes place within the framework of the project "Transformation of regions through common smart solutions", which is implemented by the Ukrainian Institute of International Policy in partnership with the Donetsk Regional State Administration and the Luhansk Regional State Administration with the assistance of the USAID Project "Economic Support of Eastern Ukraine".

The regional coordinator for smart specialization Nataliya Grishina together with the team presented the results of the previous work. During the meeting, experts from the Ukrainian Institute for International Politics and the EU Science Hub - Joint Research Center together with the participants discussed the results of the SWOT analysis and helped the Donetsk region team to form a SWOT matrix at the intersection of two industries: "Engineering and IT".

Director of the Ukrainian Institute for International Politics, Nadiya Afanasyeva, spoke about Poland's experience. Thus, the industry of the future was chosen as a priority in the Velikopol district. Mechanical engineering plays a key role in the economy of this region, both in terms of added value and employment. On the one hand, the region has very deep-rooted traditional industries: the production and repair of machines, the production of automobiles and transport equipment, metalworking, the production of furniture, wood and paper, as well as chemical production. This potential was used to transform deep-rooted traditional industries into the industry of the future, as well as to develop high technologies in this field.

Yurgita Petrauskiene, an independent expert of the Joint Research Center of the European Commission (JRC), emphasized the importance of smart specialization for the Donetsk region and the search for innovative solutions that can improve the region's economy. Based on the results of the meeting, a decision was made on further work in the direction of "Mechanical engineering and IT", and the next meeting was planned for the end of June. Further, the work will be based on researching narrow niches and holding cross-sectoral meetings.

Entrepreneurial discovery process(EDP) is a process of identification or generation of new and specific products, technologies or services that have an innovative component in broad sectors of economic activity. Thanks to EDP, the practical implementation of smart specialization in the regions of Ukraine takes place, taking into account their competitive advantages.
